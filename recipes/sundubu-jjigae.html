Sundubu-jjigae

Description

Sundubu-jjigae is a popular Korean stew known for its deep, spicy flavor and comforting warmth. The dish's main ingredient is sundubu, which is soft, uncurdled tofu that adds a pleasingly mild and creamy contrast to the spicy broth. The stew is typically made with a rich base of beef or seafood stock and includes a variety of additional ingredients such as onions, zucchini, and mushrooms. Depending on the recipe, it might also contain seafood like shrimp or clams, or meats such as pork or beef. A key component of the broth is gochugaru (Korean chili powder) or gochujang (chili paste), which imparts a vivid red color and fiery heat to the dish. Other seasonings like garlic, sesame oil, and soy sauce are added to enhance the umami and complexity of the flavors.

Traditionally served in a heated earthenware pot to keep the stew simmering throughout the meal, Sundubu-jjigae is often accompanied by a raw egg that diners can crack into the pot just before eating. As the egg gently cooks in the hot stew, it thickens the broth and adds a silky texture. This dish is not only cherished for its robust flavors but also for its nutritional value, providing a hearty, protein-rich meal. Sundubu-jjigae is typically enjoyed with a bowl of steamed rice and a selection of banchan (Korean side dishes) like kimchi, which complement the stew's spiciness and round out the dining experience.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • Soft Tofu (Sundubu): 1 tub (about 14 to 16 ounces), available at Asian grocery stores
  • Meat or Seafood: 100-200 grams (about 1/2 pound), such as shrimp, clams, or thinly sliced pork or beef
  • Vegetables:
    • 1 small zucchini, sliced
    • 1 medium onion, sliced
    • 2-3 shiitake mushrooms, sliced (you can also use other mushrooms like oyster or button)
    • 2 green onions, chopped
    • 1 chili pepper, sliced (optional for extra heat)

Broth Ingredients:

  • Stock: 2 cups of anchovy stock, beef stock, or seafood stock
  • Seasoning:
    • 1-2 tablespoons of gochugaru (Korean chili powder)
    • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste), optional for added depth
    • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
    • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
    • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
    • Salt (to taste)

Additional:

  • 1 Egg (optional, to be cracked into the stew before serving)
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar (optional, balances the flavors)

For Serving:

  • Cooked rice, to serve alongside
  • Extra green onions and sesame seeds for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare the Broth:

    • Make the Base Broth: In a pot, bring 2 cups of your chosen stock (anchovy, beef, or seafood) to a boil. If you're starting from scratch, you can make a quick anchovy stock by boiling dried anchovies and kelp in water for about 15 minutes, then strain.

  2. Sauté the Ingredients:

    • Sauté the Aromatics: In a separate pan or in a Korean stone pot (if you have one), heat a tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onions, and sauté until they start to soften and become fragrant.
    • Add Protein: Add your choice of meat or seafood to the pan and cook until it starts to brown. If using seafood like clams or shrimp, you might shorten the cooking time to avoid overcooking.

  3. Assemble the Stew:

    • Combine Ingredients: Add the sliced zucchini, mushrooms, and any additional vegetables to the pot. Stir in the gochugaru (chili powder) and gochujang (chili paste) if using, mixing well to coat the ingredients evenly.
    • Pour in Broth: Add the hot stock to the pot. Let the mixture come to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 5-10 minutes to let the flavors meld together.

  4. Add the Tofu:

    • Add the Tofu: Carefully add the soft tofu in large chunks to the pot. You can break the tofu gently with a spoon. Let the stew simmer for another 5-7 minutes so the tofu can absorb the flavors of the broth.

  5. Final Touches:

    • Season the Stew: Adjust the seasoning with soy sauce, salt, and a teaspoon of sugar if needed. The stew should have a balanced spicy and savory flavor.
    • Add an Egg: Just before serving, crack an egg into the center of the stew. Cover the pot for a minute or two to let the egg poach gently in the hot broth.

  6. Serve:

    • Garnish and Serve: Garnish with chopped green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. Serve the stew piping hot directly in the pot (if possible) alongside a bowl of steamed rice.
